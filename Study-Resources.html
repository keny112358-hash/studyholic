Biological and Biochemical Foundations

  • Question: Which organelle is the primary site of ATP production in cells?
  • Options:
    • A. Nucleus
    • B. Mitochondria
    • C. Ribosome
    • D. Endoplasmic reticulum
  • Answer: B
  • Explanation: Mitochondria produce ATP through cellular respiration, providing energy for the cell.

Chemical and Physical Foundations

  • Question: Water boils at what temperature at sea level?
  • Options:
    • A. 50°C
    • B. 75°C
    • C. 100°C
    • D. 212°C
  • Answer: C
  • Explanation: At sea level, water boils at 100°C or 212°F, which is standard knowledge for chemistry and physics.

Psychological, Social, and Biological Foundations

  • Question: Which part of the brain is primarily responsible for memory formation?
  • Options:
    • A. Cerebellum
    • B. Hippocampus
    • C. Amygdala
    • D. Medulla oblongata
  • Answer: B
  • Explanation: The hippocampus consolidates short-term memory into long-term memory, a basic neuroscience fact.

Critical Analysis and Reasoning Skills

  • Question: If a passage argues that exercise improves mood, which statement would support this claim?
  • Options:
    • A. Exercise is harmful to physical health.
    • B. Studies show exercise increases endorphins that improve mood.
    • C. Most people dislike exercising.
    • D. Mood is not affected by physical activity.
  • Answer: B
  • Explanation: Option B provides evidence that directly supports the argument that exercise improves mood.
