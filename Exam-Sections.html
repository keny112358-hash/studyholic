MCAT Foundational Concepts and Skills

Below are an assortment of codes that the MCAT is organized into. These show the detailed knowledge areas tested on the MCAT. Each code corresponds to a foundational concept that be encountered on the exam.

Biological & Biochemical Foundations of Living Systems

Foundational Concept 1: Biomolecules - Unique properties determine structure, function, and processes necessary for life.

  • 1A: Structure and function of proteins and their constituent amino acids
  • 1B: Transmission of genetic information from the gene to the protein
  • 1C: Transmission of heritable information and genetic diversity
  • 1D: Principles of bioenergetics and fuel molecule metabolism

Foundational Concept 2: Assemblies of molecules, cells, and organs - Interact to carry out organism functions.

  • 2A: Assemblies of molecules, cells, and groups of cells within single and multicellular organisms
  • 2B: Structure, growth, physiology, and genetics of prokaryotes and viruses
  • 2C: Processes of cell division, differentiation, and specialization

Foundational Concept 3: Complex systems - Sense environments and maintain internal stability.

  • 3A: Structure and functions of nervous and endocrine systems coordinating organ systems
  • 3B: Structure and integrative functions of main organ systems

Chemical & Physical Foundations of Biological Systems

Foundational Concept 4: Physical principles - Living organisms transport materials, sense environment, and respond to changes.

  • 4A: Translational motion, forces, work, energy, equilibrium in living systems
  • 4B: Importance of fluids for circulation, gas movement, and exchange
  • 4C: Electrochemistry and electrical circuits
  • 4D: Interaction of light and sound with matter
  • 4E: Atoms, nuclear decay, electronic structure, and chemical behavior

Foundational Concept 5: Chemical principles - Govern molecular interactions and reactions.

  • 5A: Unique nature of water and its solutions
  • 5B: Nature of molecules and intermolecular interactions
  • 5C: Separation and purification methods
  • 5D: Structure, function, and reactivity of biologically relevant molecules
  • 5E: Principles of chemical thermodynamics and kinetics

Psychological, Social, & Biological Foundations of Behavior

Foundational Concept 6: Perception and response - Biological, psychological, and sociocultural factors shape how individuals perceive and respond to the world.

  • 6A: Sensing the environment
  • 6B: Making sense of the environment
  • 6C: Responding to the world

Foundational Concept 7: Behavior and behavior change - Biological, psychological, and sociocultural factors influence behavior and change.

  • 7A: Individual influences on behavior
  • 7B: Social processes that influence human behavior
  • 7C: Attitude and behavior change

Foundational Concept 8: Self and social interaction - Factors influence self-perception and interaction with others.

  • 8A: Self-identity
  • 8B: Social thinking
  • 8C: Social interactions

Foundational Concept 9: Cultural and social differences - Influence well-being and social experiences.

  • 9A: Understanding social structure
  • 9B: Demographic characteristics and processes

Foundational Concept 10: Social stratification - Access to resources influences well-being.

  • 10A: Social inequality

MCAT Skills Tested

  • Skill 1: Knowledge of Scientific Concepts and Principles - recall, define, and apply scientific knowledge
  • Skill 2: Scientific Reasoning and Problem-Solving - apply knowledge to solve problems, make predictions, and evaluate cause-and-effect
  • Skill 3: Reasoning About Research Design and Execution - evaluate methodology, variables, sampling, and ethics in studies
  • Skill 4: Data-Based and Statistical Reasoning - interpret data, identify patterns, and draw conclusions from tables, charts, and experiments
